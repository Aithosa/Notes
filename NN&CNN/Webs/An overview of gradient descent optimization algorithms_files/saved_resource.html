                            Sebastian Ruder Mod Carlton Banks • a year ago

                            A big factor in escaping saddle points is how your model looks like. If you have activation functions such as sigmoid or many chained multiplications such as in vanilla RNNs, your gradients either get very small or very large and you have trouble escaping the saddle point.
                            Another factor is your learning rate: If it is too small, then SGD will also struggle to escape the saddle point.

                                    Sebastian Ruder Mod Carlton Banks • a year ago

                                    As long as you decay the learning rate of SGD, the algorithm is bound to converge to a local minimum. As the gradient you're getting with SGD is noisy, this helps you in theory to escape saddle points and poor local minima.
                                    I think the main problem here is towards the end of training: In the beginning, a large learning rate and the noisiness of SGD should make it difficult to get stuck at a saddle point/local minimum; towards the end of training, when you've decayed your learning rate and it is very small, it will get harder to escape from these. Here is a post that might shed some more light on escaping saddle points: http://www.offconvex.org/20...

                                                                                                                                          Sebastian Ruder Mod Jayant Sahewal • a year ago

                                                                                                                                          Hey Jayant,
                                                                                                                                          Thanks for your question! There's always some confusion around the signs of the gradient for the update. In order to update your parameters, you always _subtract_ the gradient or the momentum term v_t from the parameters \theta. So to get the next parameters for our NAG update, we also use the negative sign to subtract the previous momentum vector v-{t-1} from our current parameters. Hope that made things clearer.
